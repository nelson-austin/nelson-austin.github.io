    Cheyenne Frontier Days

    Dancing, drumming, and other demonstrations of Indian life at the Indian Village on the rodeo grounds at the Cheyenne Frontier Days celebration. The Western celebration that is viewed by an estimated 200,000 onlookers, including throngs of tourists, includes one of the West's most celebrated rodeos, American Indian pageantry, free pancake breakfasts, several Old West-themed parades, and other events.

    Cactus Wren

    As the state bird of Arizona, the Cactus wren is the largest wren in the United States. Its plummage is brown, with black and white spots as markings.

    Hence Rapid in the Grand Canyon National Park

    Hence Rapid is a powerful whitewater rapid formed by debris from flash floods that tore through Red Canyon. This side canyon is composed of red Hakatai Shale, with dark shafts of volcanic rock called diabese cutting through it.

    V Bar Heritage Site in Verde Valley, Coconino National Forest

    This is believed to be a solar calendar used by the Sinaguan people who resided in the area between approximately 1150 and 1400 AD. The panel features over 125 petroglyphs, including a variety of animals, people, and geometric shapes. As the sun progresses across the sky during the winter solstice, sunlight and shadow create a illuminated dart-like pattern that aligns with spirals on panel marking the winter solstice.
